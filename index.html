Let's Vespa!

The fashion of mooving

Vespa’s various models have influenced young fashion designers with its youthful spirit. Collaborations with designers who share Vespa’s core tenets of originality and out-the-box mentality have resulted in successful collaborations that represent carefree fun.

Vespa, women, and colour

Vespa, women, and colour are three elements that go together. The 1980s, more than any other decade, saw women become the main riders of Vespa. As part of a larger revolution, women took charge of their fashion by using clothing to express themselves through outfits with super-charged colours. This was the decade when women’s fashion aimed to provoke and turn heads.

The start of an adventure

To express what makes Vespa so revolutionary and innovative, requires beginning at the origin of the brand. Focusing on the year 1946 – when Vespa was founded – reveals all the elements which continue to make this scooter an icon of style.

Vespa’s creator, Corradino d’Ascanio, was an ingenious designer with a technology-first mentality who blended creativity and mechanical engineering into a functional, aerodynamic and simple vehicle.
